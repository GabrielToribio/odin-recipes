Japanese Curry

Ingredients

  • 2 onions
  • 2 carrots
  • 3 potatoes
  • 1 tsp of ginger (grated)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 1/2 lb. of your meat of choice (sub with tofu/mushrooms for vegan options)

For the Curry Sauce

  • 1 1/2 Tbsp neutral oil (for cooking)
  • 4 cups chicken stock/broth
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 package Japanese curry roux

For serving

  • 8 servings of cooked Japanese short-grain rice

Instructions

Preparing the Ingredients

  1. Cut vegetables
  2. Mince garlic and grate ginger
  3. Prepare and season meat

Cooking the Curry

  1. Heat 1 1/2 Tbsp neutral oil in a large pot over medium heat and add the onion
  2. Saute unions, stirring occasionally, until they become translucent and tender (approx. 5 mins)
  3. Add minced garlic and grated ginger
  4. Now add the meat, stirring frequently, until it's no longer pink on the outside
  5. Add stock
  6. Add honey, soy sauce, and ketchup
  7. Add carrots and potatoes
  8. Simmer on medium low heat for 15 mins, covered, or uncovered if broth covers the ingredients
  9. Once boiling, use a fine mesh strainer to skim the scum and foam from the surface of the broth
  10. Continue to cook until a wooden skewer goes through carrots and potatoes

Adding the Curry Roux

  1. Turn off heat, and put 1–2 cubes in a ladleful of cooking liquid. Slowly let it dissolve with a spoon or chopsticks and stir into the pot to incorporate. Repeat with the rest of the blocks, 2 cubes at a time.
  2. Simmer uncovered, on medium-low heat, stirring frequently, until the curry becomes thick for about 5-10 mins

Serving

  1. Serve with steamed Japanese short-grain rice on the side. Garnish with fukujinzuke (Japanese red pickled vegetables), if desired
