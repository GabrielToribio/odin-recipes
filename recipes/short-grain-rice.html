Short-Grain Rice on Stovetop

Tips Before Starting

  • Make sure to wash the rice with cold water to remove excess starch
  • Unlike pasta, short-grain rice won't be boiled, so the ratio of rice to water is very important. Measuring with a rice cooker cup or scale is recommended
    • Soak the rice for at least 30 minutes before cooking. This will allow it to cook more evenly

Equipment

  • Strainer
  • Large non-stick saucepan
  • Rice paddle

Ingredients

  • 300 grams (~1 1/2 US cups) of short-grain rice
  • 1 2/3 cups (400 ml) of water

Instructions

  1. Put the rice in a sieve and wash it in cold water until the water runs mostly clear.
  2. Drain the rice well and ad it to a pot with high sides and a lid.
  3. Add the water and cover the pot with the lid. Let it soak for at least 30 minutes.
  4. When the rice is done soaking, turn on the stove to high heat and bring the water to a boil. Do not open the lid once you've turned the heat on.
  5. Once the water comes to a full boil, turn the heat down as low as possible and let it simmer for 10 minutes.
  6. Turn of the heat and let the rice steam for another 10 minutes.
  7. Once the rice is done steaming, open the lid and use a wet rice paddle or spatula to fluff the rice.
